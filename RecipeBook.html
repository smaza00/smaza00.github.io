Recipe #1: Almond M&M Cookies

  • Time: 29 minutes
  • Serves: 15-20 servings
Ingredients Quantity
All purpose flour 1 1/2 cups
Baking powder 1 teaspoon
Baking soda 1/2 teaspoon
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Unsalted butter 1/2 cup
Granulated sugar 1/2 cup
Brown sugar, lightly packed 1/3 cup
Egg 1
Almond extract 1 teaspoons
M&M's or a mixture of M&M's and chocolate chips 1 1/2 cups

Step 1: Preheat over to 350°. Line 2 baking sheets with either silicone matts or parchment paper.
Step 2: Whisk the flour, baking powder, baking soda and salt together. Set aside for now.
Step 3: In a different bowl beat the butter and sugars for 2 minutes or until it's light and fluffy. Then add together the eggs as well as almond extract. Until fully combined.
Step 4: Mix both mixtures together.
Step 5: Mix in the M&Ms or other add-ons you want.
Step 6: Put the cookie doug onto the pans. Remember the dough will spread out.
Step 7: Bake for 8-11 minutes. Take out of oven and let cool.

Recipe #2: Almond Fudge Brownies

  • Time: 35-50 minutes
  • Serves: 16 slices
Ingredients Quantity
Unsalted butter, melted and cooled 1 cup
Vegetable oil 2 tablespoons
White sugar 1 1/4 cups
Packed light brown sugar 1 cups
Eggs 4
Almond extract 1/4 tablespoon
Salt 3/4 teaspoon
All purpose flour 1 cup
Unsweetened cocoa powder 1 cup
Roughly chopped chocolate or large chocolate chips or M&Ms 7 oz

Step 1: Preheat oven to 350°. Line a pan with parchment paper or spray a pan with oil.
Step 2: Combine butter, oil and sugars together in a bowl. Whisk it together. Then add in the eggs and almond extract; beat until the color is lighter which should be another minute.
Step 3: Mix flour, cocoa powder and salt together in a small bowl.
Step 4: Slowly the flour mixture to the other ingredients. Just mix it together. (Over mixing it affects the texture.)
Step 5: Mix in the the chocolate chips or M&Ms (which ever you choose). Don't mix it too much or it'll affect the texture.
Step 6: Pour the batter into the pan and smooth out the top.
Step 7: Bake the brownies for 25-30 minutes (fudgier brownies) 35-40 minutes (firm brownies).

Recipe #3: No-Bake Chocolate Peanut Butter Bars

  • Time: 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Serves: 24 bars
Ingredients Quantity
Unsalted butter, melted and cooled 1 cup
Creamy peanut butter 1 cup
Powdered sugar 2 cups
Graham cracker crumbs 2 cups
Package semi-sweet chocolate chips (For the chocolate topping) 12 ounces
Creamy peanut butter (For the chocolate topping) 1/4 cup

Step 1: Put foil or parchment paper on a pan.
Step 2: Combine together the butter as well as peanut butter inside a large mixing bowl. Then, add in the powdered sugar; mix until well combined. Finally, combine 1 cup of graham crackers at a time until fully combined.
Step 3: Start scooping the peanut butter mixture into the pan, then press down until the layer is even.
To make the chocolate topping:
Step 1: Add the chocolate chips along with the the peanut butter into a microwave-safe bowl. Microwave it 20-30 times, stirring well after each time. Do this until it is completely melted and smooth.
Step 2: Pour the melted mixture on top of the peanut butter layer and spread it out evenly.
Step 3: Cover the pan tightly and refrigerate it for at least an hour and half or until the chocolate topping is set.
Step 4: Take the dessert out of the pan, slice the bars, and enjoy.
